2. Computational crystal structures

Some of the properties of crystalline solids depend on the crystal structure of the materials, the way atoms, ions, or molecules are spatially arranged. There are many different possible crystal structures, and they are divided into seven different crystal system according to unit cell geometry. These seven crystal systems are cubic, tetragonal, hexagonal, orthorhombic, rhombohedra, monoclinic, and triclinic. Among them, this study targets the face-centered cubic (FCC) and diamond cubic belonging to the cubic crystal structure, and wurtzite belonging to the hexagonal crystal structure.
